The LYKA Lamp

A functional, 3D-printed lamp powered entirely by a Microbial Fuel Cell and household compost.

Renewable Energy

Generates electricity from the natural decomposition of organic waste, offering a sustainable power source.

3D-Printed Design

The lamp body and MFC housing were designed in SolidWorks and 3D-printed with ABS filament for a custom, integrated structure.

Energy Storage

Features a custom-built step-up DC converter and battery storage system to provide hours of continuous light.

From Concept to Creation

1. Concept & Research

The project began with researching the feasibility of Microbial Fuel Cells (MFCs) for household energy and conceptualizing a practical, everyday application: a self-powering lamp.

2. CAD Modeling

Using SolidWorks, I designed the lamp body and a specialized housing for the MFC components, focusing on structural integrity, aesthetics, and ease of assembly.

3. Circuit Development

A custom step-up converter and battery storage circuit, featuring a TP4056 charger module, was prototyped and soldered to efficiently manage and store the low-voltage energy produced by the MFC.

4. Fabrication & Assembly

The SolidWorks models were 3D-printed using ABS filament. The electronic components, MFC, and a 9V LED bulb were integrated into the final lamp structure.

Key Specifications

  • Primary Software: SolidWorks
  • Materials: ABS Filament
  • Electronics: TP4056 Charger Module
  • Circuit: Custom Step-up DC Converter
  • Power Source: Microbial Fuel Cell (MFC)
