The Aquatracker

A mechanical and electronic system designed to monitor water usage, created for an engineering school project.

Mechanical Design

Leveraged SolidWorks to design and model all mechanical components, translating conceptual ideas into precise 3D models ready for fabrication.

Circuit Layout

Designed the integrated circuit layout to manage sensors and data output, ensuring all electronic components worked together seamlessly within the physical constraints.

Data Monitoring

The device was conceptualized to track and display water consumption data, providing users with actionable insights into their usage patterns.

From Concept to Creation

1. Problem Definition

The project began by identifying the need for a user-friendly device to monitor household water usage as part of an engineering course challenge.

2. SolidWorks Modeling

I used SolidWorks to create detailed 3D models of the device's enclosure and internal mechanical parts, focusing on a compact and manufacturable design.

3. Electronic Integration

The circuit layout for the sensors and microcontroller was designed to fit within the custom enclosure, ensuring proper alignment and functionality.

Key Specifications

  • Primary Software: SolidWorks
  • Materials: ABS Filament
  • Electronics: TP4056 Charger Module
  • Circuit: Custom Step-up DC Converter
  • Power Source: Microbial Fuel Cell (MFC)
